UNIVERSITY OF MARY GRADUATE COMPETENCIES

Communication:

"Graduates demonstrate excellence in communication. Students are engaging and effective communicators, demonstrated by employing collaborative dialogue and adapting language to resonate with multiple audiences and/or cultures. Their communication reflects a strong command of their field(s) due to the accurate application of disciplinary standards and infusion of disciplinary frameworks and evidence into oral and written works."

Reflection: Throughout my clinical experiences I observed different communication styles between professors, preceptors, and interdisciplinary team members. Communication is the foundation of positive interactions, and is essential to building a strong professional relationship with patients. A deeper understanding of patients, the type of communication they feel supported with and thrive with, reflects a provider who listens and understands. Further, communication is reflected in the way a provider charts or tells the patient’s story. The patient’s story gives other healthcare providers or interprofessional team members the ability to see background, treatments and medications that were effective or not effective, and comprehensiveness. It has the ability to communicate the whole picture.

Example: Comprehensive Dictation

This dictation has the ability to address all health concerns with cumulative information throughout the years including lab values, diagnostic testing, or failed medications. Although technology can be incredibly useful and comprehensive, the ability to tell a patient’s story through the HPI with inclusive information listed above gives other providers and interprofessional team members the ability to understand a patient and their health story. Like the ability to present a patient to preceptors in a concise, organized manner, learning how to chart is an important piece of communication and gives an accurate description of communication skills.

Program Description

The Family Nurse Practitioner program at the University of Mary is a 2.7-year Doctorate of Nursing Practice program, with the ability to do a part-time program over 4 years (my program). The program demonstrates competence in health promotion, disease prevention, and illness management for individuals, families, and communities. This program incorporates servant leadership principles like truth, moral courage, and commitment grounded in the University of Mary Saint Gianna School of Health Sciences’ mission to respect and defend the dignity of the human person.
